Canoes and Kayaks 

The SGBA has two "Kiwi 3" two-person kayaks, one two person "Tarpon 130T" sit on top and three Canadian canoes that can be used by club members who have paid the £40 annual fee for the use of club-owned boats.

The canoes and kayaks are stored behind the boat house at the Quarry at Mill Point. They are clearly marked with the "Owned by the SGBA" sticker. You will need to know the SGBA combination lock code to access them and the boat house itself, where the paddles are stored. An additional child's seat for the kayaks is also stored in the boat house.

Members are responsible for providing and wearing their own Lifejackets and buoyancy aids.

Children can use the kayaks provided they are accompanied by an adult (in either a kayak, SGBA motorboat or another suitable craft).

The canoes and kayaks must not be dragged along the foreshore and care should be taken when grounding and launching not to damage the hulls. Any damage or loss to the boats needs to be reported to the Bosun or Quartermaster.

Canoe storage

If you have your own canoe or kayak, you can apply for foreshore storage on the SGBA canoe racks. Please contact the Dinghy Park Officer for more information.
